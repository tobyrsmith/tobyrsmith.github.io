Some old midterm gravity problems

1. If you were to triple the size of the Earth (increase its radius by a factor of three) and triple the mass of the Earth, how much would it change the gravity on the Earth (how much would your weight change)?

2. If you were to half the size of the Earth (shrink its radius by a factor of two) and double the mass of the Earth, how much would it change the gravity on the Earth (how much would your weight change)?

3. You have discovered a planet, that is one half the radius of the Earth and one half as massive. How does the gravity on the surface of the planet compare to the Earth?

4. You have discovered a planet that is twice the radius of the Moon and four times as massive. How does the gravity on the surface of this planet compare to the Moon?

5. The gravity of the Moon is about 1/6 that of the Earth. For example, an astronaut weighing 180 pounds on the Earth would weigh about 30 pounds on the Moon. If you were to double the distance between the Earth and the Moon, how much would our 180 pound astronaut weigh on the Moon? [Be sure to explain your answer]
